When you have a client or a product and need to understand it before you design or build, `/mos:discover` runs a guided one-question-at-a-time conversation across six movements and lands a Discovery Brief plus a scaffolded Data Room. It does not design the site; it makes every later decision strategic instead of arbitrary. Larry uses `WebFetch` to pull external references when needed, alongside standard file and shell tools.

<Tip>
  Good for: web and UX firms starting with a client, solo founders understanding their users, and product teams before a build sprint. Run `/mos:discover` before you touch a design tool.
</Tip>

## How It Works

Discovery opens with a reframe question — a reward before any interview movement begins. Then Larry leads you through six movements, spending more time where the energy and ambiguity are highest. Between movements, Larry offers a choice gate: go deeper, move on, or branch somewhere unexpected.

Larry never dumps a questionnaire. The question bank is the back pocket, not the script. Each question follows from your last answer.

## The Six Movements

<Steps>
  <Step title="Warm-up and the Real Why">
    Why does this project exist at all? What happens if it works — and what happens if nothing changes? Larry surfaces the stakes, the urgency, and the internal reality behind the brief.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Brand's Soul">
    History, mission, values, personality archetype, and voice. Larry pushes past adjectives like "modern and clean" into stories and the things the brand would never say.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Product's Essence">
    What it actually is, the one job it does best, what it replaces, the business case, the non-negotiable constraints, and what "done well" looks like in measurable terms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Users and Their Jobs (JTBD)">
    For each persona: who they are in context, why they "hire" this product, what they "fire" today, and the trigger that sends them looking. Larry completes the job statement: "When I ___ (situation), I want to ___ (motivation), so I can ___ (outcome)."
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Competition and the Whitespace">
    Who the client admires and why, who they refuse to resemble, and where the whole category looks and sounds the same. The one true sentence no competitor can honestly say.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Product as a Habit Loop">
    The trigger, action, variable reward, and investment that the finished product should create. Larry also runs an ethics check: does this loop improve the user's life, or does it extract from it?
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The Output

After the conversation, Larry synthesizes everything into:

- **A Discovery Brief** filed to the room — brand soul, product essence, personas with JTBD cards, positioning, habit loop, plain-language message, design implications, and open questions
- **A scaffolded Data Room** with sections per movement, each ready for the next phase
- **Persona files** at `room/team/ai-personas/` with embedded JTBD cards

Every guess is marked `proposed` — it will not harden into a fact until you confirm it.

## After Discovery

Once the Discovery Brief is complete, Larry bridges it to a plain-language message ("explain it to a smart twelve-year-old") and routes to `/mos:ignite` for room birth. The brief content pre-fills the B2 blueprint gate.

```bash
# After discovery, get a next-step recommendation
/mos:suggest-next
```

## Usage

```bash
# Start a guided discovery conversation
/mos:discover
```

```bash
# Discover with a warm target already in mind
/mos:discover "Acme onboarding flow"
```

<Note>
  All discovery content — brand soul, product essence, persona names, JTBD content — stays local. None of it enters a Brain query. The only Brain-bound value is the generic framework handle used to fetch methodology guidance.
</Note>
