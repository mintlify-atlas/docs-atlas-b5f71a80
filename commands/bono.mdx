When you have a wicked question and need a structured argument rather than one opinion, `/mos:bono` spawns a **governed research swarm** across the subdomain × hat grid — each persona bound to its hat's scrutiny discipline and its own wired sources — then debates the readings as a sequential inter-hat argument over a graph-proposed what-if hypothesis. The navigator confirms the topic, the hypothesis, and the ruling at three decision gates; the MECE-Minto synthesis files only on APPROVE and closes the loop into the room's logical graph. This is not a brainstorm; it is a structured governed debate where every claim must be supported by evidence the hat was wired to at research time.

## Usage

```
/mos:bono
```

No arguments. Larry opens with a Shape F.1 selector to confirm the topic, scope, and debate purpose before any research fires. The three decision gates — topic-confirm, hypothesis-confirm, and ruling — are the cost brakes: the research fan does not launch until the grid is scoped, and the debate does not begin until the what-if hypothesis is anchored.

<Note>
  `/mos:bono` is not `autonomous_safe`. It halts at three navigator decision gates and will not proceed without your confirmation at each one. No web research fires until after topic-confirm.
</Note>

## The six hats

Each hat is a distinct scrutiny discipline — not a personality, a lens:

| Hat | Focus | Discipline |
|-----|-------|------------|
| White | Facts & data | Cite-or-retract — only verifiable claims, all sourced |
| Red | Emotions & intuition | Instincts and gut reads — no justification required |
| Black | Caution & critical | ACH disconfirming-first — argues what could go wrong |
| Yellow | Optimism & benefits | Evidence-backed value — argues what could go right |
| Green | Creativity & alternatives | Provocation-marked — generates alternatives and inversions |
| Blue | Process & meta | Anti-convergence judge — ensures the debate stays rigorous |

<Tip>
  Black hat is always re-derived, never reused from a prior run. This is a hard anti-ossification guard — the critical perspective must be fresh for every debate.
</Tip>

## What happens

<Steps>
  <Step title="Topic-confirm gate (Gate 1)">
    Larry renders a Shape F.1 selector gathering: the **scope** (domain / subdomain grid the swarm fans over), the **purpose** (decision, thesis stress-test, or reframing), the **substrates** (which evidence sources the cells may reach), and a **hypothesis seed** Larry surfaces from the room graph. Before rendering, Larry reads the active JTBD via `lib/hmi/jtbd-state.cjs` — a `validate-idea` job sharpens the grid toward the risky assumption; an `explore` job widens it. The navigator picks; the selection becomes a typed edge. No research fires until this gate clears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Domain decomposition">
    Larry runs Engine 1 decomposition over the confirmed scope to build the subdomain grid. Each subdomain becomes one axis of the subdomain × hat cell grid the parallel fan-out runs over.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Governed team assembly">
    Larry assembles the persona roster using the Wave-2 library-first `assembleTeam`: confirmed `SyntheticExpert` nodes first, generated gaps second, with three anti-ossification guards — a mandatory fresh slot, Black always re-derived, and a reuse cap of K < N. Each persona slot then binds to `HAT_GOVERNANCE[hat]`'s scrutiny discipline. `assertHeterogeneity` runs over the assembled cells — if two cells share an identical lens, the duplicate slot is re-drawn before any research fires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Per-persona parallel research fan">
    The Wave-4 parallel `runCellFanout` launches over the subdomain × hat grid. Each cell runs `extractContext` → `runSourceLens` → `wireAccept`. A persona may not assert beyond its own wired source set. The `part8-egress-guard` is the fail-closed pre-egress gate: only the generic subdomain handle crosses toward the public web — LOCAL venture content never leaves the room.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hypothesis-confirm gate (Gate 2)">
    The flow halts so you can confirm or edit the graph-proposed what-if hypothesis. This is the anchor the debate argues over. The confirmed hypothesis becomes the `hypothesisId` all per-hat derived relationships bear on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Governed sequential debate">
    The collected cell array seeds the Wave-5 `runDebate`. Each hat argues its stance over its slice of the collected cells, governed by `enforceGovernance` and `validateCitations` — no hat may argue beyond its wired sources. The debate is sequential and never re-runs the research fan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ruling gate (Gate 3)">
    Larry halts and emits a ruling verb: `supported`, `rejected`, `refined`, or `undecided`, plus a residual-tension record naming what the debate could not close.
  </Step>
  <Step title="MECE-Minto synthesis">
    Larry applies Pyramid + MECE discipline to emit the conclusion as a `governing_thought` (≤ 250 chars) with 3–5 key claims. The `/mos:map-unknowns` Rumsfeld matrix produces the unknowns base — the open questions the debate could not close.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the loop and file">
    One `writeCloseLoop` call carries claims, relations, the conclusion, knowns, unknowns, and opportunities. The artifact files to `room/solution-design/`. Every node is born `review_status: proposed` — the engines propose, the navigator ratifies.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Output / Artifacts

The command produces a governed synthesis document filed to solution design:

```
room/solution-design/<topic-hash>/<timestamp>.md
```

The artifact contains:
- **Per-hat research findings** — each hat's sourced claims from the research fan
- **Inter-hat debate record** — the sequential argument chain with governance notes
- **Ruling verdict** — `supported | rejected | refined | undecided` with residual tensions
- **MECE-Minto synthesis** — governing thought + 3–5 key claims
- **Unknowns matrix** — open questions from the debate, from the Rumsfeld base
- **Opportunity nodes** — `proposed` opportunity entries for the bank

## Pipeline position

In the PWS methodology chain, `/mos:bono` takes a scoped topic and returns a governed synthesis:

```
/mos:map-unknowns unknowns → /mos:bono (what-if hypotheses as debate axes)
/mos:analyze-needs jobs    → /mos:bono (customer jobs as debate scope)
/mos:bono synthesis        → /mos:jtbd (active job signal updated by ruling)
```

The Engine 1 domain decomposition inside `/mos:bono` builds the subdomain grid automatically from the confirmed scope at Gate 1 — no prior command needs to have produced sub-domains first.

## Example

```
/mos:bono
```

Larry opens Gate 1. You confirm: scope is *enterprise procurement cycles in B2B SaaS*, purpose is *stress-test the hypothesis that 60-day procurement timelines are a myth for sub-$50K deals*. Larry seeds the hypothesis from a room entry.

After the research fan completes across four sub-domains × six hats, Larry halts at Gate 2. You confirm the what-if: *"What if procurement timelines compress to under 30 days when the champion has budget authority?"* The debate runs. Black hat finds two disconfirming studies. White hat cites three confirming deal-log analyses. At Gate 3, Larry rules: **refined** — the hypothesis holds for champion-led deals but fails for committee-reviewed ones.

Filed synthesis: governing thought + 4 key claims + 3 open unknowns routed to the unknowns matrix.
